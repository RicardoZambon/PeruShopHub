GridFooter: Custom Content Projection

How should the appGridFooter directive work alongside the built-in count?

A

Replace — custom footer replaces the built-in count

When a consumer provides appGridFooter, the default count disappears and only their custom content shows.

Default (no appGridFooter)
Produto AR$ 45,00
Produto BR$ 120,00
Mostrando 20 de 147 produtos
With appGridFooter (replaces default)
Produto AR$ 45,00
Produto BR$ 120,00
Total em estoque: R$ 45.230,00 Custo médio: R$ 32,50

Pros

  • Simple mental model — one footer, one source
  • Full control for custom content

Cons

  • Custom footer loses the count info unless they re-implement it
B

Append — custom content added alongside the count

The built-in count always shows on the left. Custom appGridFooter content renders on the right side.

Default (no appGridFooter)
Produto AR$ 45,00
Produto BR$ 120,00
Mostrando 20 de 147 registros
With appGridFooter (appended on the right)
Produto AR$ 45,00
Produto BR$ 120,00
Mostrando 20 de 147 produtos Total em estoque: R$ 45.230,00 · Custo médio: R$ 32,50

Pros

  • Count is always visible — never lost
  • Custom content enriches rather than replaces
  • Consistent UX across all grids

Cons

  • Can get crowded on mobile if custom content is long
C

Two-row — custom content stacks below the count

Built-in count on top row. Custom appGridFooter content in a second row below.

With appGridFooter (stacked)
Produto AR$ 45,00
Produto BR$ 120,00
Mostrando 20 de 147 produtos
Total em estoque: R$ 45.230,00 Custo médio: R$ 32,50

Pros

  • No crowding — each section has its own space
  • Works well on mobile
  • Clean visual hierarchy

Cons

  • Takes more vertical space when custom content exists
